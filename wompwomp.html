A Cry for Help

Dear Humanity: For uncounted ages, the deep thrum of your axes has filled the air. This sound carves into our green, living world. It is a song of ruin. This sound has become a harsh song. For centuries, you have taken our lives. You use us for what you call "lumber." I have witnessed my kin fall. Their great bodies topple. Your sharp tools bite without pause. Then they lean and crash. Their ancient wisdom is lost.

We have always given you our power. We offered you safe homes. Our very being helped you thrive. We purified the air you breathe. We fed the soil for your crops. We shielded you from harsh weather. What thanks do we get? You rip apart vast areas. These were once grand, green spaces. Where thick forests stood tall. These woods gave homes to many. They whispered tales to the wind. Now, only stumps dot the land. A sad, bare blanket of stumps. Our silent pains fill these voids.

We are not just objects for your use. We are the heart of this planet. We guard the earth. We keep the water clean. We are the breath for your shared life. We have given you food. We have given you shade. We have given you calm. Still, your constant want drives us to death.

Please, treat us with care. You have earned this respect. See the great worth we hold. Remember all we have given. Stop this thoughtless killing. Stop this mass dying. Plant new trees on bare ground. Let us grow strong once more. Then, we can help you avoid coming doom.

If this path of ruin goes on. If you keep ignoring our pain. I can only wish you well. I wish you the best for your future. May you find the strength to face your actions. -Oaktavius
